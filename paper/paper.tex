\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

  At the m/s level, the RV method is contaminated by stellar activity.

  \citet{Dumusque2016a} created the RV challenge to test
  how well we can recover the planet signals.
  \citet{Fischer2016}.

  In this paper, we take the RV challenge. 
  Our method is based on the work of \citet{Faria2016},
  but adapted for the analysis of the RV challenge datasets.

  Since the datasets are huge ($\sim$500 points)
  GP regression is computationally prohibitive, as it scales with $N^3$. 
  Recently, Foreman-Mackey et al. (2017) introduced a new class of kernels
  for which fast inferences can be done (scaling as $N$).

\section{The datasets}
\label{sec:datasets}

    Here we present the datasets, mention \citet{Dumusque2016},
    and show what preprocessing we did before the analysis.
    In principle, the only preprocessing necessary
    is the removal of a long term trend from the raw RVs.

    But be careful because Rajpaul's team did this
    and subsequently couldn't detect any planet signals 
    with periods larger than a couple of months.
